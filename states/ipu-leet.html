1. What is IPU CET Lateral Entry?

The **Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University Common Entrance Test (IPU CET)** is conducted annually by **GGSIPU, Delhi**. It has a dedicated code for lateral entry admissions into B.Tech courses (typically CET Code 128 for Diploma holders and Code 129 for BSc graduates).

Securing a good rank in IPU CET grants direct second-year (third semester) B.Tech admissions in multiple top government and private engineering institutes affiliated with GGSIPU in Delhi and NCR regions.

2. Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must fulfill the following parameters to qualify for GGSIPU lateral entry admissions:

  • Academic Qualification: Must have passed a 3-year minimum Diploma course in any discipline of Engineering or Technology from a recognized AICTE-approved board (like BTE Delhi) with at least 60% aggregate marks.
  • Domicile Reservations: GGSIPU maintains an **85% seat reservation** policy for Delhi region candidates (students who passed their qualifying diploma exam from a Delhi-located institute), while 15% of seats are open to Outside-Delhi candidates.
  • Age Limit: The maximum age limit for applying to GGSIPU B.Tech admissions is 21 years as of the date of admission.

3. Exam Pattern & Syllabus Weights

IPU CET for Lateral Entry is a Computer Based Test (CBT) consisting of **100 Multiple Choice Questions** (MCQs) for 400 total marks. The duration is 2.5 hours. The marking scheme is +4 marks for a correct response and **-1 mark** for every incorrect attempt.

4. Top Engineering Colleges under GGSIPU for Lateral Entry

Earning a high rank in IPU CET opens up seats in top engineering campuses in Delhi:

  1. USICT Delhi (University School of Information, Communication and Technology) — The premier GGSIPU main campus, highly coveted.
  2. MAIT Delhi (Maharaja Agrasen Institute of Technology) — Top private college in Delhi, outstanding computing placements.
  3. MSIT Delhi (Maharaja Surajmal Institute of Technology) — Extremely reputed, located in Janakpuri, excellent rankings.
  4. BPIT Delhi (Bhagwan Parshuram Institute of Technology) — Highly respected institution with strong placement trends.
  5. AIACTR Delhi — Top government campus (now part of NSUT East Campus).

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Are other-state diploma holders eligible?
Yes. Under the Outside-Delhi quota, 15% of seats are open to all students across India. The reservation category (Delhi vs Outside-Delhi) is decided strictly based on where you completed your 3-year diploma.
Is there negative marking in the IPU CET exam?
Yes. GGSIPU follows a +4 and -1 marking scheme. Skip questions you are not confident about to avoid losing marks.
