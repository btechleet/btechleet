1. What is the PULEET Exam?

The **Panjab University Lateral Entry Entrance Test (PULEET)** is conducted annually by **Panjab University, Chandigarh**. It is the exclusive portal for engineering diploma holders to gain direct second-year (third semester) B.Tech admissions.

A high rank in PULEET unlocks seats in prestigious departments like the **University Institute of Engineering and Technology (UIET)** and **Dr. S.S. Bhatnagar University Institute of Chemical Engineering & Technology (UICET)** on the main campus in Chandigarh.

2. Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must satisfy the following terms to qualify for PULEET admissions:

  • Academic Qualification: Must have passed an AICTE-approved 3-year minimum Diploma course in any discipline of Engineering or Technology from a recognized board with at least 60% aggregate marks (55% for SC/ST/BC categories).
  • Academic Continuity: Candidates must have cleared their diploma examinations with no outstanding backlogs at the time of online document verification and seat counseling.
  • Open Quota: Admissions are open to all students across India. Both general and reserved seat quotas are maintained as per Panjab University rules.

3. Exam Pattern & Syllabus Weights

PULEET is a Pen-and-Paper OMR test comprising **75 Multiple Choice Questions** (MCQs) for 75 total marks. The duration of the exam is 1 hour and 40 minutes. The marking scheme is +1 mark for a correct response and **-0.25 mark** for every incorrect attempt.

4. Top Engineering Colleges under Panjab University

The leading departments accepting PULEET ranks on the Chandigarh campus are:

  1. UIET Chandigarh (University Institute of Engineering and Technology) — The premier department, highly coveted for IT, CSE, and ECE branches.
  2. Dr. SSBUICET Chandigarh — Legendary academic reputation for Chemical Engineering and Food Technology.
  3. UIET PUSSGRC, Hoshiarpur — Reputed regional campus in Punjab offering CSE, IT, and ECE seats.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Does PULEET have negative marking?
Yes. PULEET applies a negative marking of -0.25 mark for every wrong answer. It is advisable to skip questions you are completely uncertain about to preserve your score.
Where are the exam centers for PULEET?
The PULEET offline exam is conducted exclusively at designated academic departments within the main Panjab University campus in Sector 14, Chandigarh.
