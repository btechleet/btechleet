1. What is the Kerala LET Exam?

The **Lateral Entry Test (LET)** is conducted by the **Directorate of Technical Education (DTE), Government of Kerala**. It is designed to select candidates holding a 3-year Diploma in Engineering or Technology for direct admissions to the 2nd year (3rd semester) of the 4-year B.Tech programs in APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University (KTU) affiliated colleges in Kerala.

A high rank in Kerala LET allows students to compete for highly subsidized seats in prestigious state government engineering colleges and government-aided campuses.

2. Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must satisfy the following terms to qualify for LET admissions in Kerala:

  • Academic Qualification: Must have passed a 3-year Diploma in Engineering or Technology from a recognized State Board of Technical Education with at least 45% aggregate marks (40% for candidates belonging to SC/ST categories).
  • Age Criteria: The maximum age limit as of the date of application is 30 years (with a relaxation of 3 years for SC/ST candidates).
  • Residency: Candidates must satisfy Kerala nativity guidelines to qualify for government seats (e.g. producing valid residency/nativity certificates).

3. Exam Pattern & Section Marks

The Kerala LET is a single-session offline/online test comprising **120 Multiple Choice Questions** (MCQs) carrying 3 marks each. There is a negative marking of **-1 mark** for every incorrect answer. The duration of the exam is 2 hours.

4. Top Engineering Colleges Accepting LET in Kerala

Securing a top rank in Kerala LET allows access to highly reputed public and private institutions:

  1. CET Trivandrum (College of Engineering, Trivandrum) — Premier institute with excellent placement rankings.
  2. TKM College of Engineering, Kollam — Highly reputed government-aided engineering college.
  3. GEC Thrissur (Government Engineering College, Thrissur) — Outstanding faculty and lab infrastructure.
  4. MEC Ernakulam (Model Engineering College, Kochi) — Known for top-tier computer science placements.
  5. NSS College of Engineering, Palakkad — Highly respected government-aided institution.

5. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is the LET exam syllabus common for all diploma disciplines?
Yes. In Kerala LET, all candidates sit for a single common paper containing basic math, electrical, electronics, civil, mechanical engineering, and mechanics/graphics—regardless of their specific diploma branch. This makes all-round general technical prep very important.
What is the negative marking weight?
For every correct response, +3 marks are awarded, while -1 mark is deducted for every incorrect attempt. Unanswered questions fetch 0 marks.
